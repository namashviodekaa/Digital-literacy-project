The Social Media Success Checklist
The Do's: Build Your Brand
* Audit your privacy settings regularly: Platforms change their terms and settings often. Periodically check who can see your posts and who can tag you in photos.
* Network with intention: Follow industry leaders, alumni from your college, and organizations you admire. Social media is a powerful tool for finding internships and "hidden" job opportunities.
* Think before you "Like" or Share: Your engagement history is often public or visible to friends. Ensure the content you interact with aligns with the person you want to be perceived as professionally.
* Showcase your wins: Did you finish a big project, win an intramural game, or volunteer? Sharing these moments shows you are well-rounded, active, and capable.
* Give credit where it’s due: If you’re sharing someone else’s art, research, or ideas, always tag the original creator. It shows integrity and respect for intellectual property.
The Don'ts: Protect Your Future
* Don't post during class or work: It might seem harmless, but timestamped posts during "on the clock" hours can signal a lack of focus to professors or internship coordinators.
* Don't engage in "Sub-tweeting" or drama: Publicly venting about a difficult professor or a roommate might feel cathartic in the moment, but it often makes you look unprofessional or difficult to work with.
* Don't share sensitive personal info: Avoid posting your specific location in real-time (wait until you leave the cafe), your student ID number, or your physical address.
* Don't rely on "Stories" to disappear: Screen recordings and screenshots are permanent. Never post something under the assumption that it will truly be gone in 24 hours.
* Don't post content involving illegal activity: This sounds obvious, but even "parody" posts or photos where you are a bystander to underage drinking or drug use can lead to disciplinary action from your university.
